# Overdue Patient Tracking

## Overview
Automatically identifies patients who are overdue for required device reports based on their implant type and last report date.

## Who Can See This

**Administrators:**
- See ALL overdue patients across the entire system
- Access via Admin Dashboard → "Overdue Reports" card

**Doctors:**
- See only YOUR assigned patients who are overdue
- Access via Doctor Dashboard → "Overdue Reports" card

## Report Schedule Requirements

### Defibrillator Patients
- In Clinic reports: Every 6 months
- Remote reports: Every 6 months

### Pacemaker Patients  
- In Clinic reports: Every 12 months
- Remote reports: Every 12 months

## Understanding Urgency Levels

| Status | Days Overdue | Action Needed |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Critical** | 90+ days | Immediate attention required |
| **Urgent** | 31-90 days | Schedule appointment soon |
| **Due** | 1-30 days | Schedule routine appointment |
| **Never Reported** | No previous report | Schedule initial report |

## Dashboard View

The Overdue Patients card displays:
- **Patient name and MRN**
- **Device type** (⚡ Defibrillator or Pacemaker)
- **Report type needed** (In Clinic or Remote)
- **Last report date** and days elapsed
- **Urgency badge** (color-coded)

## Taking Action

1. **Review the list** of overdue patients on your dashboard
2. **Click patient name** or "View Patient" button
3. **Schedule appropriate report** based on device type and overdue status
4. **Create the report** when patient is seen

## Features

- **Pagination**: View 10 patients per page
- **Real-time updates**: List refreshes as reports are completed
- **Direct access**: Click through to patient profiles
- **Visual indicators**: Color-coded urgency badges and device icons

## Tips for Managing Overdue Patients

**For Administrators:**
- Monitor system-wide overdue trends
- Identify patients who may have slipped through the cracks
- Use for quality assurance and compliance tracking

**For Doctors:**
- Check your overdue list weekly
- Prioritize Critical and Urgent patients
- Coordinate with staff to schedule appointments

## Why This Matters

Regular device monitoring is critical for:
- **Patient safety** - Detecting device issues early
- **Regulatory compliance** - Meeting reporting requirements
- **Device longevity** - Optimizing battery and therapy settings
- **Clinical outcomes** - Ensuring appropriate therapy delivery
